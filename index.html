Research

As a general overview, my research at UCR's ARCS (Autonomous Robots and Control Systems) Lab involves the designing of an autonomous smart pressure chamber system to be mounted on a robot for field testing of leaf water potential of crops. This method of measuring leaf water potential is one of the least invasive and most practical in terms of both finance and the technology readily available. A manipulator selects and cuts a leaf from the crop of interest, and the leaf is inserted into the pressure chamber for water potential measurement. The measurement is taken automatically by a pressure sensor once the computer vision system detects a sap drop at the tip of the xylem.

Delving into greater detail, the focus of my research within the Agrobotics team has primarily been to develop a computer vision control system to take leaf water potential measurements from various crops using the Scholander pressure chamber. This research aims to automate the dangerous task of taking the LWP measurements manually, to increase the efficiency of the task, and to provide a scalable method to take LWP measurements over a wide variety of crops. LWP measurements are significant in agriculture becuase they determine the irrigation schedule of the crops. My line of work also involves the collection of a novel image dataset of wet and dry xylems from a couple different species of plants. My group intends to publish this dataset publicly and we are currently working on the CASE 2022 conference paper as of early March 2022 for this.
